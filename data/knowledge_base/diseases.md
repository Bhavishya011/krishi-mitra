# Common Crop Diseases — Organic Remedies
# ==========================================
# Source: ICAR / KVK / ZBNF literature (public domain)
# Region focus: North India (Haryana, Punjab, UP, Rajasthan)


## Tomato Early Blight (Alternaria solani)

**Affected Crop:** Tomato (Tamatar)
**Symptoms:** Dark brown to black concentric ring-shaped spots (target board pattern) appear on older lower leaves first. Leaves turn yellow around the spots and eventually dry up and fall. Fruits may develop dark sunken spots near the stem end. The disease progresses from bottom of the plant upward.
**Favorable Conditions:** Warm humid weather (24-29°C), heavy dew, frequent light rains, overcrowded planting.
**Urgency:** This Week — treat within 3-5 days to prevent spread to upper canopy.

**Organic Remedy:**
1. **Neem Oil Spray** — Mix 5ml neem oil + 1ml liquid soap in 1 litre water. Spray on both sides of leaves every 7 days.
2. **Jeevamrit Drench** — Apply 200 litres jeevamrit per acre as soil drench to boost beneficial soil microbes that suppress Alternaria.
3. **Trichoderma Application** — Mix 2kg Trichoderma viride in 100kg well-decomposed FYM, broadcast near root zone.
4. **Cultural Practice** — Remove and destroy infected lower leaves immediately. Maintain 60cm spacing between plants. Use mulching to prevent soil splash onto leaves.


## Tomato Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)

**Affected Crop:** Tomato (Tamatar), Potato (Aloo)
**Symptoms:** Water-soaked irregular dark patches on leaves that rapidly expand, especially during cool wet weather. White fuzzy mold visible on underside of leaves in early morning. Stems develop dark brown streaks. Fruits show large, firm, dark, greasy-looking patches. Entire plant can collapse within days.
**Favorable Conditions:** Cool nights (10-15°C) with warm days (20-25°C), high humidity above 90%, prolonged wet weather, fog.
**Urgency:** Today — this disease spreads extremely fast. Act immediately.

**Organic Remedy:**
1. **Bordeaux Mixture (organic-approved)** — Mix 100g copper sulphate + 100g lime in 10 litres water. Spray as preventive every 10 days during risk periods. This is the strongest organic option for Phytophthora.
2. **Pseudomonas fluorescens** — Spray 20g per litre water on leaves every 10 days. Acts as a biocontrol agent.
3. **Cultural Practice** — Destroy all infected plants immediately by burying or burning (do not compost). Ensure proper drainage. Avoid overhead irrigation. Stake plants for better air circulation.
4. **Preventive** — Use disease-resistant varieties like Arka Rakshak, Arka Samrat.


## Wheat Brown Rust (Puccinia triticina)

**Affected Crop:** Wheat (Gehun)
**Symptoms:** Small, round to oval, orange-brown powdery pustules scattered randomly on the upper surface of leaves. Pustules release rusty powder when touched. In severe cases, leaves dry up prematurely reducing grain filling. Does not follow veins (unlike stripe rust).
**Favorable Conditions:** Moderate temperatures (15-22°C), high humidity, dew formation, late-sown wheat more susceptible.
**Urgency:** This Week — treat before pustules spread to flag leaf which affects grain yield severely.

**Organic Remedy:**
1. **Neem Kernel Extract** — Soak 500g neem kernels overnight in 10 litres water. Filter and spray on crop every 10 days.
2. **Panchagavya Spray** — Mix 3% panchagavya solution (30ml per litre water). Spray at 15-day intervals. Boosts plant immunity and suppresses fungal growth.
3. **Buttermilk Spray** — Mix sour buttermilk (chhachh) 1:10 with water. The lactic acid bacteria suppress rust spores on leaf surface.
4. **Cultural Practice** — Use resistant varieties (HD-2967, HD-3086). Avoid excessive nitrogen. Ensure timely sowing (November 1st week for North India).


## Wheat Yellow/Stripe Rust (Puccinia striiformis)

**Affected Crop:** Wheat (Gehun)
**Symptoms:** Yellow-orange powdery pustules arranged in stripes along the leaf veins, giving a striped appearance. Appears first on lower leaves and progresses upward. In severe epidemics, entire fields look yellow from a distance. More damaging than brown rust as it affects younger leaves.
**Favorable Conditions:** Cool weather (10-15°C), cloudy conditions, high humidity, early-sown or irrigated wheat.
**Urgency:** Today — stripe rust can destroy up to 100% yield if it reaches the flag leaf. Report to nearest KVK immediately.

**Organic Remedy:**
1. **Same as Brown Rust remedies** — Neem kernel extract, panchagavya, buttermilk spray.
2. **Trichoderma Foliar Spray** — 4g Trichoderma per litre water, sprayed at first sign of pustules.
3. **Critical** — For stripe rust, varietal resistance is the most important defense. Grow recommended resistant varieties from your state agriculture university.
4. **Cultural Practice** — Destroy volunteer wheat plants (self-sown from previous season). Balanced nutrition — avoid excess nitrogen. Early sowing in endemic areas.


## Mustard Aphid (Lipaphis erysimi)

**Affected Crop:** Mustard (Sarson), Rapeseed (Toria)
**Symptoms:** Small, soft-bodied, greenish-grey insects clustered on tender shoots, flower buds, and underside of young leaves. Plants appear stunted with curled and distorted leaves. Heavy honeydew secretion causes black sooty mold on leaves. Severe infestation during flowering causes poor pod formation and shrivelled seeds.
**Favorable Conditions:** Cool dry weather (15-22°C), cloudy but rainless periods in January-February, late sowing.
**Urgency:** This Week — aphid populations double every 3-4 days in favorable weather.

**Organic Remedy:**
1. **Neem Oil Spray** — 5ml neem oil + 1ml soap per litre water. Spray twice at 7-day intervals targeting shoot tips and undersides of leaves.
2. **Tobacco Decoction** — Soak 500g tobacco waste in 4 litres water for 24 hours. Strain, add 100g soap flakes, dilute to 40 litres. Spray in evening hours.
3. **Yellow Sticky Traps** — Place yellow sticky traps (5-6 per acre) to monitor and reduce flying aphid populations.
4. **Biocontrol** — Conserve natural predators — ladybird beetles (coccinellids) and syrphid fly larvae are voracious aphid feeders. Avoid broad-spectrum sprays that kill these beneficials.
5. **Cultural Practice** — Timely sowing (October 15-25 for North India) helps the crop escape peak aphid season. Intercrop with wheat or gram which harbor aphid predators.


## Paddy Blast (Magnaporthe oryzae)

**Affected Crop:** Paddy / Rice (Dhan / Chawal)
**Symptoms:** Diamond-shaped or spindle-shaped spots with grey center and dark brown border on leaves. Spots enlarge and may kill entire leaves in severe cases. Node blast causes blackening of nodes and stem breakage (neck blast). Neck blast is most devastating — panicle neck turns black, grains become chaffy and unfilled.
**Favorable Conditions:** High humidity (>90%), temperature 25-28°C, prolonged leaf wetness, excessive nitrogen application, close spacing.
**Urgency:** Today — neck blast during panicle emergence can destroy the entire harvest. Leaf blast must be controlled before booting stage.

**Organic Remedy:**
1. **Pseudomonas fluorescens** — Seed treatment (10g/kg seed) + foliar spray (20g per litre) at tillering and booting stages.
2. **Trichoderma** — Soil application with FYM at transplanting. Foliar spray 4g per litre at 15-day intervals.
3. **Silicon Application** — Apply rice husk ash (200kg/acre) in field. Silicon strengthens cell walls and reduces blast severity.
4. **Cultural Practice** — Use resistant varieties (Pusa Basmati-1, PRH-10). Avoid excess nitrogen — use balanced NPK. Maintain 20x15cm spacing. Drain fields intermittently instead of continuous flooding.


## Chilli Leaf Curl (Geminiviruses — transmitted by whitefly)

**Affected Crop:** Chilli (Mirch), Capsicum (Shimla Mirch)
**Symptoms:** Young leaves curl upward and inward, becoming thick, leathery, and brittle. Plant becomes stunted with shortened internodes giving a bushy appearance. Flowers drop prematurely. Fruits are small, deformed, and few. Whiteflies visible as tiny white flying insects on leaf undersides when plant is shaken.
**Favorable Conditions:** Warm dry weather (25-35°C), whitefly populations peak in September-October and February-March.
**Urgency:** This Week — once symptoms appear, that plant cannot be cured. Focus on preventing spread to healthy plants.

**Organic Remedy:**
1. **Neem Oil Spray** — 5ml per litre water, targeting whitefly on leaf undersides. Spray every 5-7 days during whitefly activity period.
2. **Yellow Sticky Traps** — Install 8-10 traps per acre at canopy height. Replace every 2 weeks.
3. **Verticillium lecanii** — Spray 5g per litre water in evening hours. This entomopathogenic fungus infects and kills whiteflies.
4. **Cultural Practice** — Remove and destroy virus-infected plants immediately to prevent spread. Use silver mulch (reflective) to repel whiteflies. Grow maize or sorghum as border/barrier crop around chilli field.
5. **Companion Planting** — Interplant with marigold (genda) which repels whiteflies.


## Brinjal Fruit and Shoot Borer (Leucinodes orbonalis)

**Affected Crop:** Brinjal / Eggplant (Baingan)
**Symptoms:** Caterpillar bores into tender shoots causing them to wilt and droop (dead heart in shoots). In fruiting stage, larvae bore into fruits leaving round entry holes with frass (excrement). Infested fruits are unfit for market. Internal tunneling causes fruit rot. Can damage 60-80% fruits if uncontrolled.
**Favorable Conditions:** Warm humid weather, continuous cropping of brinjal, peak during kharif season (July-October).
**Urgency:** This Week — regular monitoring and action needed throughout the fruiting season.

**Organic Remedy:**
1. **Pheromone Traps** — Install Leucinodes orbonalis pheromone traps (5 per acre) to trap male moths and monitor population. Replace lures monthly.
2. **Neem Seed Kernel Extract (NSKE)** — Spray 5% NSKE (50g neem kernel per litre water) at 10-day intervals during fruiting.
3. **Trichogramma Release** — Release Trichogramma chilonis egg parasitoid cards (6-8 cards per acre, 50,000 eggs per card) at 15-day intervals during flowering.
4. **Cultural Practice** — Collect and destroy infested shoots and fruits weekly (most critical practice). Do not leave fallen fruits in the field. Clip wilted shoots 5cm below the damage point.
5. **Trap Crop** — Grow Solanum indicum (wild brinjal) as trap crop around the field — borer prefers it over cultivated brinjal.

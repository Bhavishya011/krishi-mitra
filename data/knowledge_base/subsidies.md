# Government Subsidy Schemes for Farmers
# =========================================
# Source: Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Government of India
# Focus: Schemes relevant to organic/natural farming in North India
# Note: Amounts and eligibility as of 2024-25 guidelines


## PM-KISAN — Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi

**Full Name:** Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana
**Hindi:** प्रधानमंत्री किसान सम्मान निधि योजना

**What it provides:** Direct income support of ₹6,000 per year to eligible farmer families, paid in 3 equal installments of ₹2,000 every 4 months, directly to the farmer's bank account (DBT — Direct Benefit Transfer).

**Eligibility:**
- All landholding farmer families across India
- Land should be in the farmer's name (or spouse's name for women beneficiaries)
- No minimum land size requirement
- Exclusions: Government employees, income tax payers, professionals (doctors, engineers, lawyers, CAs drawing salary), institutional landholders

**How to apply:**
- Visit nearest Common Service Centre (CSC) / Jan Seva Kendra
- Or apply online at pmkisan.gov.in
- Documents needed: Aadhaar card, bank passbook, land records (khatauni/patta)

**Current status:** Over 11 crore farmers benefiting. Installments are credited in April-July, August-November, December-March cycles.


## PKVY — Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana

**Full Name:** Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (Traditional Farming Development Scheme)
**Hindi:** परम्परागत कृषि विकास योजना

**What it provides:** Financial assistance of ₹50,000 per hectare over 3 years to farmer groups for adopting organic farming. This includes:
- ₹31,000 per hectare for organic inputs (bio-fertilizers, bio-pesticides, organic manure, compost, vermi-compost, botanical extracts)
- ₹8,800 per hectare for value addition and marketing/distribution of organic produce
- ₹3,000 per hectare for cluster formation, training, and capacity building
- Remaining for certification and administrative costs

**Eligibility:**
- Farmers must form a cluster/group of 50 or more farmers (or 50 acres under one group)
- Each farmer needs at least 1 acre under organic cultivation
- Cluster must agree to adopt organic farming for minimum 3 years
- Priority to SC/ST farmers, women farmers, and small/marginal farmers

**How to apply:**
- Contact District Agriculture Officer or Block Agriculture Officer
- Form a farmer group and register under PKVY through the state agriculture department
- Third-party organic certification (PGS — Participatory Guarantee System) is provided free under the scheme

**Key benefit for demo:** This scheme directly supports the kind of natural farming (ZBNF) that Krishi Mitra recommends. Farmers following our organic remedies can simultaneously get financial support through PKVY.


## Soil Health Card Scheme

**Full Name:** Soil Health Card Scheme
**Hindi:** मृदा स्वास्थ्य कार्ड योजना

**What it provides:** Free soil testing and a personalized Soil Health Card for every farm holding. The card provides:
- Soil nutrient status: N, P, K, S, Zn, Fe, Cu, Mn, Bo
- Soil pH and Electrical Conductivity
- Organic carbon content
- Crop-specific fertilizer recommendations based on actual soil deficiencies

**Eligibility:**
- All farmers in India — no restrictions
- Cards issued every 2 years (one cycle covers all holdings)

**How to get your card:**
- Contact nearest Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or Agriculture Department office
- Or check online at soilhealth.dac.gov.in with your Aadhaar or card number
- Soil samples collected by trained staff from the farmer's field
- Results available within 2-3 weeks

**Relevance to natural farming:** The Soil Health Card helps identify which nutrients are deficient, so the farmer can target their organic inputs (e.g., if zinc is low, apply zinc-enriched compost rather than blanket application). It validates the improvement in soil organic carbon that ZBNF practices like jeevamrit and mulching bring over time.


## NMSA — National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture

**Full Name:** National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture
**Hindi:** राष्ट्रीय सतत कृषि मिशन

**What it provides:** Umbrella mission with multiple sub-schemes promoting climate-resilient and sustainable farming:

### Key Components:
1. **Rainfed Area Development (RAD):** Promotes integrated farming systems in rainfed areas — multilevel cropping, agroforestry, livestock integration. Provides ₹25,000-50,000 per hectare for adopting integrated farming models.

2. **On Farm Water Management (OFWM):** Subsidies for micro-irrigation (drip and sprinkler):
   - 55% subsidy for small/marginal farmers
   - 45% subsidy for other farmers
   - Additional 10% from state government in many states

3. **Soil Health Management (SHM):** Supports setting up vermi-compost units, bio-fertilizer production, and organic input production at village level.

**Eligibility:** All farmers — priority to small and marginal farmers, SC/ST, women farmers. Apply through District Agriculture Officer.

**Relevance:** The RAD component specifically supports multilevel cropping — exactly what Krishi Mitra educates farmers about. Farmers can get financial support for transitioning to the layered farming systems we recommend.


## Haryana Organic Farming Incentive

**Full Name:** Haryana State Organic Farming Policy / Prakritik Kheti Khushhal Kisan Yojana
**Hindi:** प्राकृतिक खेती खुशहाल किसान योजना (Haryana specific)

**What it provides:**
- ₹25,000 per acre incentive for farmers practicing natural/organic farming (in some districts)
- Free training in ZBNF/natural farming techniques through KVKs
- Market linkage support through organic farmer markets (weekly organic mandis in select cities)
- Certification support through PGS-India organic certification

**Eligibility:**
- Farmers in Haryana state
- Must adopt chemical-free farming on at least 1 acre
- Must attend at least one training program on natural farming
- Verification by agriculture department field officers

**How to apply:**
- Contact Block Agriculture Officer in your block
- Register through Meri Fasal Mera Byora portal (fasal.haryana.gov.in)
- Some districts have specific enrollment drives — check with local KVK

**Note:** Several other states have similar schemes — Andhra Pradesh (APCNF), Madhya Pradesh (Prakritik Kheti), Gujarat (GSNF). Krishi Mitra can inform farmers about their state-specific programs.
